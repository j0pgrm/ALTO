About ALTO

Arizona Labor Trafficking Outreach (ALTO) is a community-rooted initiative working to end labor trafficking across Arizona through outreach, training, advocacy, and survivor-centered support. We believe that every person has the right to work free from exploitation, coercion, and abuse.

Our work focuses on reaching vulnerable populations — including day laborers, migrant workers, and low-wage laborers — who are often overlooked in broader anti-trafficking efforts. Through culturally responsive outreach, partnerships with trusted community organizations, and engaging educational workshops, we equip individuals and institutions with the tools to identify and prevent labor trafficking in all its forms.

ALTO is committed to uplifting the voices of survivors, building trust across diverse communities, and creating long-term solutions through systems change and public awareness.

Meet Our Founder: Ezequiel Dominguez

Ezequiel Dominguez, MSW, is a Ph.D. student in social work at Arizona State University and the founder of Arizona Labor Trafficking Outreach (ALTO). With a deep commitment to social justice, Ezequiel's work focuses on addressing labor exploitation among underserved populations, particularly Latino day laborers and migrant workers. Drawing inspiration from his family's involvement in the United Farm Workers movement, he leads community-engaged research and outreach initiatives to raise awareness and provide resources to those affected by labor trafficking. Ezequiel's efforts have been recognized nationally, including receiving the Arizona Diamondbacks' Líderes Under 40 Award for his impactful work in the community.

Our Vision

We envision an Arizona where every worker — regardless of immigration status, language, race, gender, or background — has the right to labor with dignity, in conditions that are fair, safe, and free from fear or coercion. In our vision, exploitation is no longer normalized or overlooked. Instead, communities are empowered to recognize labor trafficking, speak out against injustice, and access the protections they deserve. We imagine a future where employers are held accountable, systems prioritize human rights over profit, and survivors of labor trafficking are not only believed but supported in reclaiming their lives.

We believe the most powerful solutions come from those most directly impacted. That's why we are committed to uplifting and working alongside immigrant, refugee, and labor communities — not just as beneficiaries, but as leaders, educators, and agents of change in this movement.Our Arizona is one where justice is not conditional. It's collective, accessible, and rooted in the belief that no one should have to sacrifice their freedom to earn a living.

Our Values

  • 🧍‍♀️🧍‍♂️ Dignity: We believe every person has inherent worth and the right to be treated with respect.
  • ⚖️ Justice: We challenge the systems and structures that enable labor trafficking to thrive.
  • 🌍 Access: We ensure information, support, and training are available in multiple languages and cultural contexts.
  • 🎤 Survivor Voice: We uplift and center the voices of people with lived experience.
  • 🤝 Collaboration: We work in partnership with other advocates, agencies, and grassroots leaders to create collective impact.
