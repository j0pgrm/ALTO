What do our trainings look like?

Our labor trafficking trainings are dynamic, evidence-based, and community-focused. Sessions are led by experienced trainers who guide participants through real-world scenarios, interactive discussions, and practical tools to recognize and respond to labor trafficking. Whether you're a service provider, employer, student, advocate, or simply want to make a difference, our trainings are designed to meet you where you are â€” in-person or virtually.

What do our trainings offer?

Our goal is to equip individuals and organizations with the knowledge and confidence to identify labor trafficking, understand risk factors, and take action. Each training includes:

  • A clear breakdown of what labor trafficking is and how it operates in different industries.
  • Common red flags and indicators of trafficking in the workplace.
  • How to respond ethically and effectively when you suspect someone is being exploited.
  • Guidance on relevant laws, victim rights, and available resources.
  • Case studies, scenario-based learning, and Q&A opportunities.

Trainings are tailored for audiences such as educators, social workers, law enforcement, medical professionals, and community organizations.

Labor Trafficking 101

This foundational course provides an overview of labor trafficking in the U.S. and Arizona. Perfect for beginners or those looking to build awareness, Labor Trafficking 101 covers:

  • What labor trafficking is (and what it isn't)
  • How it intersects with immigration, poverty, and labor laws
  • Key industries where trafficking occurs
  • Common recruitment tactics and red flags
  • How to safely report concerns or connect people with help

Ideal for: general audiences, nonprofits, student groups, and anyone interested in learning the basics.

Labor Trafficking 201

Building on the introductory training, Labor Trafficking 201 goes deeper into survivor-centered responses, legal considerations, and community-based intervention strategies. This training includes:

  • Complex case studies based on real scenarios
  • Understanding coercion and trauma-informed approaches
  • Strategies for frontline professionals and multidisciplinary teams
  • In-depth look at the H-2A and H-2B visa systems and employer obligations
  • Collaborating with law enforcement and community networks

Ideal for: direct service providers, first responders, attorneys, and professionals working closely with vulnerable populations.
